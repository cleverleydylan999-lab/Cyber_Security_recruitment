Job Overview

Lead our Threat Intelligence program and drive strategic security initiatives across our organization. As Threat Intelligence Lead, you'll oversee all intelligence operations, manage analyst teams, conduct advanced threat research, and develop threat intelligence strategies that inform our company's security posture. You'll work with C-suite executives, coordinate with law enforcement, and establish yourself as a thought leader in the cybersecurity community.

Key Responsibilities

  • Lead and manage the threat intelligence team, setting direction and strategy
  • Conduct advanced threat analysis and research on nation-state and criminal threat actors
  • Develop and maintain threat intelligence frameworks and methodologies
  • Prepare executive briefings and strategic intelligence reports for leadership
  • Coordinate with law enforcement and intelligence agencies on critical incidents
  • Manage threat intelligence platforms and tools (MISP, AlienVault, etc.)
  • Conduct forensic analysis and digital investigations
  • Develop intelligence collection strategies for specific threat actors
  • Represent the organization at security conferences and speaking engagements
  • Establish relationships with intelligence community partners and information sharing groups

Required Qualifications

  • 8+ years of cybersecurity experience with 5+ years in threat intelligence
  • Proven track record leading and managing security teams
  • Deep expertise in threat landscape, APT groups, and attack campaigns
  • Advanced knowledge of forensics, reverse engineering, and malware analysis
  • Experience with threat intelligence platforms and OSINT tools
  • Strong understanding of geopolitical factors and cyber warfare
  • Excellent communication skills for both technical and executive audiences
  • GIAC certifications (GCIH, GCIA, or GMON) or equivalent

Preferred Qualifications

  • Intelligence Community background or security clearance
  • Published research or papers in cybersecurity
  • Speaker at major security conferences
  • Experience with MITRE ATT&CK framework
  • Knowledge of attribution methodologies
